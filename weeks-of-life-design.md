# Weeks of Life - Design Overview

## Concept
"Weeks of Life" is a contemplative, minimalist mobile-first application that visualizes a user's life in weeks. Inspired by Dieter Rams' principles, the design prioritizes simplicity, usefulness, and honesty.

## Onboarding Flow
1. **Birth Date Input**
   - Prompt users to enter their birth date.
   - Optionally select estimated lifespan (default 80 years / 4,160 weeks) from demographic averages or custom input.
2. **Life Visualization**
   - Display a responsive grid of small squares representing weeks of a typical human lifespan.
   - Color or shade weeks already lived.
   - Provide pinch-to-zoom and smooth scrolling.

## Feature Enhancements
1. **Interactive Insights Panel**
   - Shows weeks lived, time left, Mondays/Saturdays passed, time since historical events, and a simple "life movie act" metaphor.
2. **Life Milestone Highlights**
   - Average ages for milestones (first kiss, graduation, marriage, retirement) visualized.
   - Option for custom events with annotations.
3. **Reflection Mode**
   - Minimal animations, ambient sounds, and rotating philosophical quotes (Seneca, Marcus Aurelius, modern designers).
   - Journaling prompts such as "What mattered to you most this year?".
4. **Save/Share Mode**
   - Export life-grid snapshots or quote overlays as image cards for reflection and optional sharing. No gamification.
5. **Ethical & Inclusive Design**
   - Colorblind-friendly, with text indicators and ARIA roles.
   - Mindfulness-focused language to avoid fatalistic tone.
   - Localization-ready.

## UI & Tone Guidelines
- Sentence case text throughout the app.
- Default dark mode with option to switch.
- Modern, readable, unobtrusive typography.
- Minimal UI: no badges, notifications, or alerts unless explicitly enabled.

## Emotional Goals
The app should be a quiet reflection tool—a philosophical mirror. It avoids productivity framing and instead encourages users to pause and consider how they spend their weeks. Everything in the interface should support stillness and clarity.
